The Israel Summit at Michigan

Co-Founder and Co-Director

November 2019

Overview

The Israel Summit at Michigan is a day-long, university-wide speaker conference focused on Israeli innovations and initiatives that are transforming the world around us every day. In addition to hosting promiâ€‹nent Israeli and American entrepreneurs and change makers, the Summit includes an Israel Career and Special Opportunities Fair, where students can engage with representatives from top Israeli companies and discover a multitude of trips to Israel for people of all backgrounds. The inaugural Israel Summit at Michigan took place on November 10th, 2019.

My Role

I spent both the Summer of 2017 and Summer 2018 living in Tel Aviv and learning about impactful Israeli innovationss. When I heard about the incredible success of the Israel Summit at Harvard (2017), I knew I needed to bring this initiative to Michigan's campus. I quickly joined forces with two other students to create the Israel Summmit at Michigan, where I served as co-Founder and co-Director.

November 10th, 2019

The Israel Summit was kicked off with two keynote speakers, Inbal Arieli, author of Chutzpah: Why Israel Is a Hub of Innovation and Entrepreneurship, and Sivan Ya'ari founder and CEO of Innovation: Africa, who uses Israeli solar technologies to transform the lives of millions in Africa. After the morning keynote speakers, students engaged in a Special Opportunities Fair, where there was information about different types of trips to Israel for people of all backgrounds. In the afternoon attendees heard from Noam Gershony, a former IDF Pilot, who become a gold medal Paralympian after being injured, Brendan Tuohey, CEO of PeacePlayers International, Allyson Greenfield, Executive Director of the Israel Cancer Research Fund in Chicago, and Neil Schwartz, a representative from IsraAid. To end the day, Edun Sela, CEO of Tikkun Olam Makers, and Jonny Caplan, CEO and Executive Producer of TechTalk Media, addressed the crowd.
